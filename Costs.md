5. Exercise Figure 5.1 is a project WBS with cost apportioned by percentages. If the total
project cost is estimated to be $800,000, what are the estimated costs for the following
deliverables?
a. Design
b. Programming
c. In-house testing
What weaknesses are inherent in this estimating approach?
Ans:
a. Design
The WBS shows that Design is allocated 40% of the total project cost.
𝐷𝑒𝑠𝑖𝑔𝑛 𝐶𝑜𝑠𝑡 = 800,000 × 40% = 800,000 × 0.40 = 320,000
So, the estimated cost for Design is $320,000.
b. Programming
The WBS shows that Programming is allocated 22% of the total project cost.
𝑃𝑟𝑜𝑔𝑟𝑎𝑚𝑚𝑖𝑛𝑔 𝐶𝑜𝑠𝑡 = 800,000 × 22% = 800,000 × 0.22 = 176,000
So, the estimated cost for Programming is $176,000.

The WBS shows that In-house testing is allocated 40% of the Implementation phase, and the

Weaknesses in This Estimating Approach:
Over-simplification: The WBS assigns fixed percentages to broad project categories without
considering the varying complexity of individual tasks. This can lead to inaccurate estimates,
as some activities may require significantly more effort, expertise, or resources than others.
Lack of Detail: The percentage-based approach lacks sufficient granularity. It doesn't provide
insight into the specific resources, time, or effort needed for each task, making it challenging
to adjust estimates as the project evolves.
Rigid Assumptions: This method relies on static percentage allocations, assuming they
remain valid throughout the project. However, as the project progresses and scope or
requirements change, these assumptions may no longer hold true.
Ignoring Risks and Uncertainties: The approach doesn't factor in potential risks or the need
for contingency planning. It fails to account for unexpected issues like delays or resource
constraints, which can significantly impact project cost and timelines.
